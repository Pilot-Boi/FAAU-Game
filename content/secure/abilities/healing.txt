ABILITY OVERVIEW // HEALING

Classification:
Biological Amplification / Regenerative Enhancement

Primary: Subject 008

Summary:
The ability designated "Healing" allows the Primary subject to accelerate the natural recovery processes of a target individual through direct contact.

Upon activation, the subject appears capable of enhancing cellular repair, stabilizing injuries, and restoring physical condition at a rate far exceeding normal biological limits.

The effect does not generate new biological material, but instead amplifies the body's existing capacity for recovery.

Mechanism:
Activation requires direct physical contact between Subject 008 and the target individual. During activation, a localized increase in regenerative activity is observed within the affected tissue.

The subject appears to stimulate cellular repair, increase metabolic activity, and stabilize damaged structures simultaneously.

The underlying mechanism responsible for this amplification remains unknown.

Observed Effects:
- Rapid closure of wounds and stabilization ofdamaged tissue
- Significant reduction in recovery time for injuries
- Temporary increase in overall physical stability of the target
- Reduction in pain response following activation

Healing effects scale with duration of contact and the subject’s level of exertion.

Limitations:
- Requires sustained physical contact with the target individual
- Effectiveness decreases when applied to severe or widespread trauma
- Repeated use places measurable strain on Subject 008
- The ability cannot restore fully destroyed or missing tissue

Security Notes:
The ability presents a unique support capability among test subjects and may be used to extend the operational lifespan of other avians.

Subject 008 should be deployed alongside other subjects to maintain operational viability.

Use of the ability should be monitored, as repeated activation may result in fatigue or diminished effectiveness in Subject 008.

Note:
Subject 008 demonstrates a consistent tendency to use this ability on other subjects without instruction.
